Your Cardiometabolic Prevention Roadmap
This summary shows where you stand today and the most important steps to lower future heart, kidney, and metabolic risk.

Where you stand:
- 10-year risk: 3.74%
- 30-year risk: 18.8%
- About 4 out of 100 similar patients may have a cardiovascular event over the next 10 years.
- Plaque status has not been measured.
- Clinician context: Level 3 (3B) - Actionable early CKM / atherogenic risk

Why risk is elevated:
- Near-term risk is borderline, but 30-year risk and multiple early risk markers support prevention discussion.
- Cholesterol particles: ApoB 92; LDL-C 126; non-HDL-C 156. These numbers help show plaque-driving cholesterol in the blood.
- Blood sugar / diabetes: A1c 6.1%. Improving blood sugar can lower long-term heart, kidney, and metabolic risk.
- Kidney protection: eGFR 82; UACR 34. Kidney markers also help estimate heart and metabolic risk.

Current goals:
- LDL-C: 126 mg/dL to <100 mg/dL
- non-HDL-C: 156 mg/dL to <130 mg/dL
- ApoB: 92 mg/dL to <90 mg/dL
- A1c: 6.1% to individualized
- Kidney / albuminuria: 34 mg/g; G2A2 to reduce albuminuria / preserve eGFR

Next steps:
1. Lower plaque-driving cholesterol: Treat toward the cholesterol goals above.
2. Protect the kidneys: Review kidney protection options with your clinician.
3. Aspirin safety: Do not start aspirin unless your clinician recommends it.
4. Additional testing: Lp(a) can be checked once to guide long-term prevention.
5. Next step: CAC may clarify plaque burden if treatment intensity remains uncertain.

This roadmap supports clinician review and shared decision-making. It does not replace medical advice.
